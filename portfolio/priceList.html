SPECIAL PACKS

  • For more than 7 characters for games, you receive one basic for free.
  • For more than 6 pages, you receive 1 free cover.

TERMS OF SERVICE

  • More details mean a higher price, so please provide a full and detailed list of your requirements.
  • Payments are always required in full upfront. Except for cases where the work takes more than one month, it can be divided in between months, that's not an issue.
    (Stick and circle pre-sketches can be provided for free if necessary before any payment.)
  • PayPal fees of 5.9% must be included in the payment. You can also purchase a slot through my Gumroad, but please let me know beforehand.
  • Delivery times will be communicated once I receive all the details. Delivery times may vary and can be weekly, biweekly, or monthly. You can decide how you want to divide the pieces as well.
  • Possible delays of up to one or two days may occur. If there are any significant delays, I will contact you.
  • Please send all details up front, as I DO NOT MAKE CHANGES OR MODIFICATIONS to sketches (such as "more of this" or "less of that") unless I have missed something and need to correct it, due to time reasons.

    A back-and-forth exchange lasting several days would force me to raise the price by up to 50%.

    If you need this, you should mention it to me so I can see if it fits into my schedule. Sometimes everything is almost fully booked.
  • Please note that I do not offer refunds, I've never needed to process one.
  • Reservations can only be made one month in advance of the date when I will actually work on the piece. This helps to avoid any issues and ensures that everything is properly arranged.

THE WORKFLOW

  • ALL commissions are placed in a queue
  • Sketches are delivered any day during the first half of the month in which your commission will be done, once approved it enters the lineart phase and I disappear because I will be working on other commissions, no advances are sent because it takes me between 2 and 4 days to finish so there is nothing to show until I get to your position in the queue.
  • Sketches won't include details like character design unless it's necessary. To save time, all details go directly into the lineart phase.
  • No need to message me often, believe me, i already put lot of pressure on myself, i have many clients, imagine if all of them pushed me at once.

ABOUT COMIC SCRIPTS

  • You don't have one and you give me complete freedom. PERFECT! just keep in mind that I don't make unpaid changes.
  • You don't have one, but you have the main idea with vague details. PERFECT!
  • You have a concise and straightforward comic script with only actions and dialogues.PERFECT!
  • You don't have one, but you have a book. Working with Books have 50% fee

    Books have a lot of text that I don't need. And the time it takes to arrange and make the layout doesn't even allow me to fit it into my schedule sometimes.
    Also i would need at least half of the project payment in advance to make it secure for me.
    You can summarize and turn it into a comic script or you can ask the AI to do it.
  • You have a comic script with camera angles and shots that is quite descriptive. I can try my best to follow it as closely as possible. But, it's important to understand that I may not be able to match your vision 100%. As mentioned before, I'm unable to go back and forth with many rounds of questions and revisions over a long period of time, since that would affect my delivery schedule for other clients. I appreciate your understanding and patience.
  • I don’t usually get involved in the story unless you’d like to add an extra fee to the project for that. If I were to provide feedback or narrative input, the price per page would be higher.

OTHER IMPORTANT DETAILS

When I have more freedom over camera angles, panel layout, and page composition—and you only provide the actions or simple camera notes—it's much easier for me to keep pages priced at $100-$150. Even with multiple characters or non-AI backgrounds, I can usually avoid going over $150 by rearranging panels, zooming in, hiding parts of characters, or shifting heavier elements around—basically treating the page like Tetris.
